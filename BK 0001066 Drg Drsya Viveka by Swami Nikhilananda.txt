Drig-Drishya Viveka as the name indicates is a Vedantic treatise of 64 verses inquiring into the nature of the 'Seer' (Drig) and the 'Seen' (Drishya) an inquiry which is of utmost importance for understanding the conclusions of the Vedanta philosophy. This brief treatise compresses much information into its body of few verses. The book contains the original text in Devanagari its word-by-word meaning in English a running translation and exhaustive notes by Swami Nikhilananda.
